Step 2 of 6

Your story in one paragraph

Imagine you're meeting a hiring manager at a coffee shop. They ask "so what do you do?" What's your answer? Don't think too hard — write it like you'd actually say it. Aim for 3–5 sentences.

example: "I've spent 12 years running a small bakery — I do everything from sourcing flour to training new hires. Lately I've been teaching myself bookkeeping software because my accountant keeps fixing my mess. I'm looking for something that lets me use the people-skills and the spreadsheet-skills at the same time."

Step 3 of 6

Each entry can be a job, a side project, a volunteer thing, a class you taught — anything. Just tell me what it was and what you actually did. Click "Add another" to keep going.

example: "Ran the front of house at Romano's Italian for 6 years. Managed 12 servers, did weekly schedules, trained every new hire."

Apps, websites, products, photos, articles, art, classes you taught, problems you fixed.

Step 6 of 6

Review & save

Here's the file the agent will read. Looks good? Save it. Want to tweak? Jump back.

What happens to my file?

Download: writes credentials.md to your browser's Downloads folder. Move it to ~/.config/jobscout/credentials.md so the agent finds it.

Save locally: visible only when you launched this wizard with python3 jobscout.py setup --web. The wizard talks to its bundled Python server and writes the file straight to the right place. Nothing leaves your machine.

Copy to clipboard: handy if you'd rather paste it into a text editor and save it yourself.
